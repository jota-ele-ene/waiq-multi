The European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA) has issued a crucial warning about the impending threat that quantum computers will pose to current encryption systems. Its latest report details the complexity of the transition to post-quantum cryptography (PQC) and highlights the lack of preparedness in many sectors. ENISA urges member states and organizations to begin evaluating their infrastructures now and to develop coordinated migration plans to avoid a 'cryptographic catastrophe' when quantum computing matures. This call to action is fundamental for Europe's digital security and technological sovereignty, directly impacting data protection, intellectual property, and economic stability in the quantum era.
