The European Commission is taking proactive steps to integrate Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs) into the legal landscape. A new study aims to analyze different DAO governance models and propose pathways for their legal recognition, which could reduce uncertainty for Web3 projects and users. The initiative seeks to balance innovation and security, offering clarity on liability, consumer protection, and taxation. By providing a clear legal framework, Europe could attract investment and talent in the Web3 space, fostering new business models and civic participation, which is essential for digital competitiveness and governance in the decentralized economy.
