As the EU advances its digital regulatory framework, DAOs find themselves at a legal crossroads. The decentralized and often unincorporated nature of DAOs clashes with traditional legal frameworks designed for centralized entities. Legal experts point out that the lack of clarity regarding member liability, the applicability of consumer protection laws, and the regulation of governance tokens is hindering the innovation and adoption of this organizational model. There is an ongoing debate about the need to adapt existing laws or create specific frameworks for DAOs that allow for their responsible flourishing, protecting participants and fostering democratic participation in the governance of Web3 projects. This situation highlights the tension between rapid technological evolution and slow regulatory adaptation.
