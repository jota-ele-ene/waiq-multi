The European Commission has unveiled a preliminary proposal for a governance framework addressing the complex interplay between Artificial Intelligence, decentralized Web3 platforms, and advancements in quantum security. This move aims to establish ethical and security standards for applications combining these emerging technologies, anticipating the challenges they pose concerning data privacy, algorithmic autonomy, and resilience against next-generation cyberattacks. The initiative stems from growing concerns about AI's use in Web3 environments, where transparency and immutability are key principles, but also where quantum vulnerabilities could undermine trust in transactions and data integrity. The proposed framework emphasizes the need to develop "quantum-safe" solutions for Web3 infrastructure and to protect AI models, while also setting guidelines for algorithmic decision-making in decentralized systems. It is expected that this framework will lay the groundwork for future specific legislation, promoting responsible innovation and safeguarding the fundamental rights of European citizens in the digital and quantum era.
