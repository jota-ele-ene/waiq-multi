The European Union's pioneering AI Act is entering its crucial implementation phases, bringing significant challenges to light. Analysts and business associations highlight the need for clearer guidelines, especially for Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs), which often lack the resources of larger corporations to adapt to the new regulation's complexity. Another critical point is the management of cross-border data flows and interoperability with third-country legislation, which could impact European competitiveness. The ongoing debate focuses on how to balance the protection of fundamental rights with the promotion of innovation, without creating an excessive bureaucratic burden that stifles technological development in the region.
