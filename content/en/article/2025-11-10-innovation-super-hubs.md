This article explores the Global Innovation Index 2025, revealing how innovation is increasingly concentrated in specific "superhub" regions around the world. It highlights the rapid rise of Asian innovation powerhouses like Shenzhen–Hong Kong, Tokyo–Yokohama, Beijing, and Seoul, contrasted with traditional centers like Silicon Valley. The piece delves into the unique ecosystems that drive these clusters—combining cutting-edge research, industrial capacity, and dynamic financial networks—while emphasizing the critical role of state support and social networks in fueling innovation. If you're interested in understanding the future landscape of global technological leadership and the factors underpinning these high-impact innovation hubs, this article offers a compelling and insightful read.
