The final approval of the AI Act by the European Parliament marks a milestone in global artificial intelligence regulation. This pioneering legislation introduces a risk classification for AI systems, from "unacceptable risk" to "minimal risk," with proportional obligations for each category. Significant emphasis has been placed on transparency, human oversight, and accountability, especially for high-risk systems used in critical infrastructure, education, and justice. In addition to protecting fundamental rights and ensuring ethical AI use, the Act also seeks to foster innovation by providing regulatory sandboxes and support for SMEs developing AI. The implementation of this law will have profound implications for businesses, governments, and citizens across Europe and will set an international precedent.
